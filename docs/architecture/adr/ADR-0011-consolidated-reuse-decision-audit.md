## Context

Afenda had already produced a TPM-only reuse audit, but the next comparison wave introduced multiple external repositories with different strengths:

- TPM is the closest match to Afenda's current runtime shape and exposes real end-to-end fragility.
- Gauzy is stronger as a TypeScript enterprise modularity benchmark.
- Akaunting is stronger as a modular-monolith and extension-economy benchmark.
- Odoo is stronger as a domain taxonomy and ERP workflow breadth benchmark.

Running these as separate peer audits would create scattered archaeology, inconsistent verdicts, and conflicting recommendations. The repo needs one canonical reuse doctrine so external comparisons strengthen Afenda's own truth and governance rather than diluting them.

## Decision summary

1. Afenda will maintain one canonical reuse audit, not multiple equal-weight repo reviews.
2. TPM remains the anchor benchmark for runtime, shell, and end-to-end integration fragility.
3. Gauzy, Akaunting, and Odoo are benchmark layers used only in defined authority lanes.
4. Afenda itself remains the final authority for truth, governance, tenancy, typing, and permissions.
5. No benchmark repo may be used as justification for direct legacy coupling into Afenda production packages.
6. Every reuse verdict must be evidence-backed and must end in an owned Afenda action with a gate.

## Architecture contract

Turn the decision into enforceable contract statements.

### Required

- One canonical reuse audit must exist for Afenda's benchmark portfolio.
- TPM must remain the runtime authority benchmark.
- Every benchmark recommendation must cite one Afenda surface and one benchmark surface.
- Every benchmark recommendation must end with an Afenda `owner` and `gate`.
- Final actions must be limited to `Borrow now`, `Rebuild in Afenda style`, or `Reject permanently`.

### Forbidden

- Parallel benchmark audits that contradict the canonical master audit.
- Direct portability recommendations from benchmark repos into Afenda production packages.
- Treating Gauzy, Akaunting, or Odoo as authorities over Afenda's truth, tenant, permission, typing, or governance law.
- Elevating `akaunting/module-my-blog` into a peer platform benchmark.

### Allowed exceptions

- Repo-local working artifacts under `.artifacts/reports/` may evolve faster than canonical docs so long as the ATC still points to the active report path.
- New benchmark repos may be added later only through an update to the canonical audit doctrine and benchmark-authority table.

## Alternatives considered

### Option A: Keep separate peer audits

- Pros:
  easy to start incrementally and low coordination overhead
- Cons:
  encourages contradictory verdicts, duplicated reasoning, and benchmark drift

### Option B: TPM-only forever

- Pros:
  stays tightly aligned to Afenda's current topology
- Cons:
  loses valuable benchmark input on modularity, extension systems, and ERP taxonomy

## Consequences

### Positive

- Reuse decisions become consistent, evidence-backed, and action-oriented.
- External repo comparisons now strengthen Afenda's own architecture instead of competing with it.
- Teams can distinguish runtime lessons from taxonomy lessons and extension-model lessons.

### Negative

- The master audit becomes a higher-maintenance artifact than a single TPM report.
- Adding benchmark repos later will require doctrine updates rather than ad hoc notes.
